About Chhath Puja

Chhath Puja is an ancient Hindu festival dedicated to Surya Dev (Sun God) and Chhathi Maiya. It symbolizes purity, faith, and devotion. Devotees observe fasting, take holy dips, and offer arghya to the setting and rising sun to express gratitude for sustaining life on Earth.

Schedule

  • Evening Arghya: Offer prayers at sunset on the terrace or near water.
  • Morning Arghya: Assemble before sunrise for the holy offering.

Rituals & Significance

  • Nahay Khay: The first day, devotees bathe in the holy river and consume only sanctified food.
  • Lohanda & Kharna: The second day involves fasting and preparing gur-kheer as a sacred offering.
  • Sandhya Arghya: The third day, devotees offer water and fruits to the setting sun.
  • Usha Arghya: The final day, offerings are made to the rising sun, marking the end of fasting and celebration of purity.
